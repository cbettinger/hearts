Hearts

Christian Bettinger

Hearts is a simple and beginner-friendly yet challenging trick-avoiding card game for two to five players. The basic object is to take as few points as possible, where Hearts and the Queen of Spades score.

This page covers a rather modern variant of the Hearts-type family of card games, which often is called American Hearts or Black Lady. Certainly also due to the fact that Microsoft has delivered a popular implementation of this modern variant under the name Hearts in many versions of its Windows operating system, many people (including me) refer to this variant when they talk about Hearts. The preceeding variant, in which the Queen of Spades does not score, is therefore referred to as Original Hearts.

Strategy

As in many card games, try to remember the cards that have already been played. Pay attention to what cards you are passed to guess the other's strategy.

Basic

  • Passing
    • Pass high cards, especially Aces, Kings and Queens.
    • Do not pass Spades lower than the Queen.
    • Do not pass low Hearts.
    • Do not pass your last Club.
  • Playing
    • Do not hesitate to take a trick with no scoring card.
    • If the trick contains scoring cards, play the highest card that will not take the trick.
    • If you have to take a trick, play your highest card.
    • Void Clubs and Diamonds as soon as possible.
    • If you have a void in the led suite, play your most dangerous card.
    • If you do not have Spades higher than the Queen, lead Spades to force others to play the Queen of Spades (a.k.a. Bleeding out Spades).
    • Do not lead with an Ace (unless the Queen of Spades has already been played).

Intermediate

  • Passing
    • If you want to lead the second trick, pass the 2 of Clubs.
    • If you want to lead the second trick, do not pass the Ace of Clubs.
    • Keep at least one high Heart (at best the Ace) to prevent others from Shooting the Moon.
    • Do not pass Spades.
